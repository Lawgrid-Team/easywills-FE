At easyWills (“we”, “our”, “us”), we are committed to protecting the confidentiality and integrity of your personal information, particularly because our services involve sensitive data related to wills, estates, family, and financial affairs.

This Cookie Policy explains how we use cookies and similar technologies on our website (https://easywills.ng) (the “Site”). It complies with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR, 2019) and the European Union General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, 2016/679).

2. What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile). They enable websites to function effectively, remember user preferences, enhance security, and provide analytics or targeted advertising.

For easyWills, cookies may process personal identifiers (such as IP address, browser type, login details) and usage data, which can be linked with the sensitive personal data you provide in the course of using our services.

3. Legal Basis for Processing (NDPR & GDPR)

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR; NDPR Part 2.1(1)(a)) – required for analytics, functionality, and marketing cookies. Consent is always opt-in.
  • Legitimate Interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR; NDPR Part 2.1(1)(d)) – for cookies strictly necessary to deliver secure services.
  • Contractual Necessity (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR) – where cookies are essential for providing the online wills services you have requested.

You may withdraw your consent at any time via our cookie banner or your browser settings. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

4. User Rights under NDPR and GDPR

You have the right to:

  • Be informed about how cookies process your data.
  • Access data linked to cookies that identify you.
  • Rectify inaccurate or incomplete cookie-linked data.
  • Erase data (“Right to be Forgotten”).
  • Restrict processing of cookie data in specific circumstances.
  • Object to non-essential cookies or profiling.
  • Data portability (GDPR Art. 20) for data collected with your consent.
  • Withdraw consent at any time.

Redress mechanisms:

  • Nigerian residents may complain to the National Information Technology Development Agency (NITDA).
  • EU residents may complain to their local supervisory authority.

6. Managing Cookies & Consent

When you visit the Site for the first time, you will see a cookie banner allowing you to:

  • Accept All Cookies (essential + non-essential).
  • Reject Non-Essential Cookies (essential only).
  • Manage Preferences (choose categories).

You can also disable cookies in your browser. Note: blocking Strictly Necessary Cookies may prevent you from logging in, drafting, or finalizing wills on the Site.

7. Data Security & Confidentiality

Because wills and estate planning involve highly sensitive information, all cookie-linked data is handled under the same security framework we apply to personal data, including:

  • End-to-end encryption of sessions.
  • Data minimization (storing only what is necessary).
  • Access restricted to authorized staff and processors under strict contractual obligations.
